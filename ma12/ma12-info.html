Two-Fold 2d3 Two-Fold 2d3 is an open multiball 13p setup based around the 2d3 setup, which is a vanilla setup with a slight random factor to determine PRs. What's fun about this is that it does this not once but twice, and generates certain PRs to block mafias, and certain PRs to block the werewolves. This puts in a fun and balanced multiball game! The setup: - 5 Vanilla Towns - 1 Goon - 1 Werewolf But that's only 7 roles with 13 players! Next, roll two d3's for both matrixes, to determine what power role the mafia and werewolf gets, and what power roles the town gets. Example: For mafia, a 2C is rolled. This means a Goon, a Jailkeeper and another Vanilla Town are added to the setup. For werewolf, a 1A is rolled. This means a Werewolf Roleblocker, a Guardian and a Seer are added to the setup, making the setup: - Jailkeeper - Guardian - Seer - 6 Vanilla Towns - 2 Goons - Werewolf Roleblocker - Werewolf Usually there are only 5 or 6 Vanilla Towns, which is less than half, making it fun for both VT haters and VT fans alike! What does each role do? I'll explain it for mafia, and next tell the werewolf counterparts. Cop - Check someone at night to figure out if they are mafia. Neopolitan - Check someone at night to figure out if they are a PR or Vanilla (VT and Werewolves are seen as Vanilla) Jailkeeper - Jail someone at night, protecting them from mafia kills and roleblocking them if they are town or mafia. Doctor - Heal someone at night, protecting them from mafia kills. Tracker - Track someone at night, figuring out who they visit. Can't track the werewolf factional kill. For werewolf roles, Cop is the same as Seer, Neopolitan is the same as Auditor, Jailkeeper is the same as Wizard, Doctor is the same as Guardian and finally Tracker is the same as Wolfstalker.
